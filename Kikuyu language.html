What is the Kikuyu Language?

The Kikuyu language, normally referred to as Gikuyu, is the mother tongue of the Kikuyu people, one of the most prosperous ethnic tribes in Kenya. The development of this language firstly originated from the Bantu migrations that happened over a millennium ago when the people speaking Bantu spread from West Africa into central and southern parts of the continent. Gikuyu is indeed rich in cultural meaningfulness; it's a vital communication among the Kikuyu people and retains their heritage, traditions, stories, and collective identity. It is therefore part and parcel of Kikuyu heritage, encompassing values, history, and beliefs of the tribe, hence instilling a sense of unity and belonging among its speakers. When speaking Gikuyu, the Kikuyu people will be contributing to the preservation for future times of their special way of looking at life and their way of doing things, enabling them to joyfully celebrate and respect their identity in an ever-changing world.
