What are the Origins of the Kikuyu Tribe?

The Kikuyu tribe, also known as Agikuyu, is the most populous ethnic group among the people in Kenya and is mainly concentrated in the central highlands. Anthropological research indicates that the Kikuyu belong to the larger group of Bantu-speaking peoples thought to have migrated from West and Central Africa to East Africa around 1,500 years ago. They are said to originate from a mythical ancestor named Gikuyu, who is presumed to be the founder of the tribe. In Kikuyu mythology, Gikuyu was given land by Ngai, the creator god, on the slopes of Mount Kenya, where he settled and began cultivating the rich land. The nine daughters born to him and Mumbi are believed to be Gikuyu's ancestors who then founded the various clans among the Kikuyu. Agriculture forms an important part in the life of these Kikuyu people, who have much attachment with the land and nature. Their religious belief focuses on the existence of an omnipotent creator god by the name Ngai, who is said to have dwelled on Mount Kenya, making this mountain a very sacred place in their culture. It is this combination of ancestor worship and respect for the creator that has defined the Kikuyu spiritual landscape and maintained their cultural identity through the ages.
